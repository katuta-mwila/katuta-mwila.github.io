Foundations Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

  1. If I truly believe in myself and maintain a growth mindset I can get anything done.
  2. That I am less clear on my identity and values than I had realized.
  3. The strength of my ego is largely determined with how comfortable I am with a problem.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values are important because they dictate why you are learning in the first place how much you prioritize learning over other things in life.

Empathy is important because you need to understand how the code you write will be understood by other people as well as understanding what other people are doing and feeling.

Self-awareness is important because you constantly need to tune the way you behave in accordance to how others are perceiving you.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

What surprised me the most was the effectiveness that understanding neuroplasticity and a growth mindset can have on your learning. This encourages me to always persist and try my best to understand things.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

The most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning was to look within and truly understand what my values are, what's my identity, how I will approach my learning plan and then put all that into sentences.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I believe the reason for the large focus on human skills is because a large part of career in tech is about how you communicate with and express yourself to others. This is important for applying for and getting a job as well as performing well once in a job.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead? Justify your answer.

In some ways I do believe studying human skills the online part of a course is difficult because there is little opportunity to put things into practice outside of digital communication.
